## Why this was the right cycle to ratify

The Evidence Layer brief v0.1 was drafted operating *as if*
candidates 4–6 were ratified. Without ratification, the brief was
load-bearing on uncommitted authority. Three paths existed:

1. Ratify and proceed (Option A — selected).
2. Defer ratification and continue with the brief operating on
   uncommitted authority indefinitely.
3. Withdraw the brief's reliance on candidates 4–6 and revise
   accordingly.

Option 1 was selected because the candidates had clear recurrence
patterns, well-scoped cross-module application, and concrete
failure modes each prevents. Wording revisions are cheaper than
ratification deferrals; if specific phrasing needs tightening
later, that's a one-line correction, not a doctrinal blocker.

◆ CLICK ANY METRIC TO UNDERSTAND IT
Every EVM row is interactive. Click BAC, CPI, VAC — or any metric — and ProjectHUD opens a detailed explainer showing the formula, the exact numbers that went into the calculation for your project, a plain-English interpretation of what the result means, and a task-level drill-down spreadsheet showing each task's Budget Cost, Actual Cost, and Variance.
◆ REAL DATA. REAL HOURS. REAL COSTS.
EVM figures aren't estimates or manual inputs. Actual Cost (AC) is computed automatically from daily task journal entries logged by your team. Budget at Completion (BAC) is locked from your project's approved budget hours. The math runs itself — you just read the results.

◆ IF YOU CAN SEE IT, YOU CAN MANAGE IT
Most project managers know which tasks are late. Far fewer know which tasks, if delayed by even one day, will push the entire project end date. That's the Critical Path — and ProjectHUD makes it visible at a glance.

Red nodes are on the critical path. Zero float. Zero tolerance. Every day of delay on a red task is a day added to your delivery date. ProjectHUD computes this automatically from your WBS and shows it as a live network diagram — phase by phase, task by task.
◆ NEAR-CRITICAL: THE WARNING ZONE
Amber nodes have 5 days of float or less — they're not yet critical, but one small disruption puts them there. ProjectHUD surfaces these automatically so your team can intervene before a near-critical task becomes a project-breaker.
◆ PERT: WHAT IF THINGS GO WRONG?
PERT (Program Evaluation & Review Technique) models schedule uncertainty using three estimates per task — Optimistic, Most Likely, and Pessimistic — to compute a probability-weighted expected duration and standard deviation.

ProjectHUD auto-seeds PERT estimates from your task durations and renders a probability distribution curve showing the likelihood of hitting key project milestones on time.
